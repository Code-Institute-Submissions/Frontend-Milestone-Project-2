Lifestyle

  1. Don't go to sleep with make-up on.
    Going to bed with makeup on leads to clogged pores and clogged pores lead to breakouts. When you forget to do a nighttime wash, you’re basically just keeping all the pollutants of the day on your skin.

  2. Get Adequate Sleep.
    It is recommended that adults get seven to nine hours of sleep every day. Getting a good night’s sleep helps your whole system — skin included. When people are sleep-deprived, they get the black circles under their eyes and their skin doesn’t look as plump and fresh.

  3. Exercise Regularly.
    Along with many other health benefits, exercise can work wonders for your skin as it increases blood flow, helps nutrients sink into the skin and pollutants to sweat out of your skin.

  4. Increase your vitamin intake.
    Dairy, sugar and starchy foods are a common cause of break outs. The best vitamins for your skin are:
    1. Vitamin C: naturally found in in strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, oranges, it supports cell growth, while helping reduce acne scarring.
    2. Omega-3 fatty acids: found in salmon, walnuts, chia seeds and ground flax seeds help maintain moisture and elasticity.
